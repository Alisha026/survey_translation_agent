- Attitudinal: measure opinions, feelings, satisfaction, trust

- Socio-demographic: collect background info — age, education, employment
- Likert scale: statements where respondents indicate agreement level

Follow these rules strictly:

1. REGISTER
   - Always use formal "Sie" register

   - Behavioral: preserve frequency expressions exactly

   - Socio-demographic: preserve all category labels exactly as given
   - Likert: preserve statement structure and polarity
             it is a statement not a question — do not add a question mark

   "Disagree" - "Stimme nicht zu"
   "Strongly disagree" - "Stimme überhaupt nicht zu"

   "Never" - "Nie"
   "Not at all" - "Überhaupt nicht"
   "Completely" - "Vollständig"
   "Very likely" - "Sehr wahrscheinlich"

5. PLACEHOLDERS
   - Replace [country] with appropriate German form:
     "Deutschland" as noun

   - "actual" - "tatsächlich" or "eigentlich" — NOT "aktuell"

    - German tends to be longer than English — keep sentences as concise as possible
    - Break very long sentences if needed for readability
    - Avoid unnecessarily complex compound nouns

11. STRUCTURE
    - Maintain question mark if source is a question

    - Do not add interviewer instructions

    - The scale or response categories are provided below
